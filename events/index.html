Carnatic Rhythm Workshop

Sriram Raman

Friday December 6, 2024

Led by NYC mridangam Guru Sriram Raman, this session introduced participants to the intricate world of Carnatic laya (rhythm). Open to all, the workshop explored the math in music, concepts behind Carnatic percussion, and provided a deeper understanding of rhythm's role in the classical tradition.

Columbia University Classical Night

CU Hindu Student Association X CU Raaga

Thrusday November 14, 2024

Classical Night was an enchanting celebration of South Asian classical arts, showcasing the rich traditions of music, dance, and storytelling. Organized by CU Raaga in collaboration with HSO, the event brought together diverse art forms and talented performers, creating a vibrant cultural experience that honored the depth and beauty of classical heritage.

One Step Closer Koncert (OSCK)

RootEd Foundation X CU Raaga

November 9, 2024

On November 9th, CU Raaga collaborated with the RootEd Foundation USA to present the Columbia edition of One Step Closer Koncerts 2024. The event featured a spectacular Bharatanatyam Margam performed by our very own planning board member, Aditi Iyer! This unique narrative performance format sparked meaningful conversations between the audience and the art form, celebrating the beauty and richness of Indian classical dance.

Aditya Ranade Concert

Aditya Ranade, Rohan Prabhudesai, Aditya Phatak

November 14, 2024

We kicked off the semester on September 21 with a spectacular Hindustani vocal concert featuring the legendary Aditya Ranade, accompanied by stalwarts Rohan Prabhudesai on harmonium and Aditya Phatak on tabla. For many, this was an introduction to South Asian classical music, particularly Hindustani shastriya-sangeet, a tradition originating from Northern India. It was a night to remember, full of soulful melodies and profound artistry.
